PERSON

A “person” is not a human being. It is a legal construct — a mask used by systems to assign duties, obligations, and liability. Corporations, trusts, and estates can all be “persons.” The living man stands behind the construct, but is not the construct itself.

When language says “any person must,” it is speaking to the entity, not the life behind it.
