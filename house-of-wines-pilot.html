What Graceful Concierge Actually Does

Graceful is a personalized reservation and guest memory layer built specifically for independent restaurants. It sits in front of the existing flow and fixes the five problems every operator knows too well:

  • Missed calls → lost tables and frustrated guests.
  • Voicemail tag → wasted time and uncertainty.
  • No-shows → unpredictable shifts and lost revenue.
  • No guest memory → no upsell and no loyalty.
  • No simple marketing engine → no way to invite guests back intentionally.

Graceful captures the reservation, remembers the guest, and uses that memory to grow check size and repeat visits — without adding complexity for staff.

The Pilot in One Glance

For 30 days, House of Wines runs with Graceful active — at no cost — to measure what actually happens to revenue, guest behavior, and table flow.

  • Duration: 30 days (calendar month or agreed window).
  • Cost: $0 for the entire pilot.
  • Scope: Reservations, guest memory, soft upsells, and reporting.
  • Risk: None — existing habits and tools stay in place.
At the end of 30 days, the question is simple:
Did covers, check size, and repeat visits improve enough to justify keeping Graceful on?

How This Pilot Increases Revenue

Graceful is designed to push on three specific levers that matter to a restaurant’s bottom line:

  • More covers: fewer missed calls and fewer lost requests.
  • Higher spend per table: calm, well-timed suggestions for specials, wine flights, and add-ons.
  • More repeat business: guests feel remembered, not processed.

The pilot is not about theory. It is about showing, in numbers, that a small layer of intelligence around reservations can produce a measurable lift in revenue for House of Wines.

What Stays the Same for House of Wines

Owners and staff tend to say “no” to anything that smells like a system overhaul. This pilot is deliberately the opposite:

  • Current point-of-sale stays the same.
  • Current floor operations stay the same.
  • Current staff habits stay the same.
  • Graceful runs quietly alongside what already works.
Key principle: Graceful does not replace the personality of House of Wines.
It simply remembers more, suggests more, and loses fewer opportunities.

What You Receive at the End of 30 Days

When the pilot ends, House of Wines receives a concise owner-level summary. The intention is not to drown anyone in data, but to answer a few practical questions clearly:

  • Total reservations handled through Graceful during the pilot.
  • Estimated revenue influenced (based on covers × average check).
  • Number of guests with meaningful profiles (preferences, occasions, etc.).
  • How many guests returned during the pilot window.
  • Which soft upsell messages were accepted most often.

All of this remains useful to House of Wines whether Graceful continues beyond the pilot or not.

The pilot is designed so that House of Wines is better informed, even in the “no” scenario. There is no downside.

The Only Real Question

With cost removed, disruption minimized, and reporting guaranteed, the remaining question for ownership is straightforward:

“Do we want to see, for ourselves, whether a concierge-level memory layer can meaningfully grow House of Wines’ revenue and guest loyalty?”

If the answer is “yes, we want to see it,” the next step is simply to choose a start date for the 30-day window.
